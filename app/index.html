About

Refugee Community is a free forum for passionate people who want to take action and help refugees. Whether you are looking to setup a Refugees Welcome group in your country, create a startup for refugees, or simply host a refugee in your home—this is a community for people who have heard the cry of our fellow humans and are determined to come together and do whatever we can to help them.

Who Is This For?

  • Good Samaritans
  • Refugees Welcome Groups
  • Makers and Doers
  • Entrepreneurs
  • Artists and musicians

We Need Your Help

  • There are 4.1 million Syrians who have sought refuge in other countries since 2011. [1]
  • As of today, 499,826 Syrian refugees have reached Europe this year[2].
  • Only 2.2% of Syrian refugees have been offered places to resettle this year. [3]
  • There are millions of dollars coming in from tech companies around the world to help with the refugee crisis. [4]
  • There has been an overwhelming response from global citizens who want to help refugees. [5][6]

We cannot do this alone.
We need to work together.

If three Dutch entrepreneurs could create an Airbnb for refugees in three days, what would happen if the brightest minds from around the world joined together in one place to talk about the problems at hand? What kind of solutions would we see?

What better opportunity for innovation than a complex problem that is facing millions of people from all over the world?

How could we break this problem into smaller parts? How can we apply the best of technology and design to solving the problems of the refugee crisis?

Whether you're an entrepreneur, a writer, designer, developer, government official, student, or just someone who wants to help—join our community and offer your help to those in need. We are determined to face the complexity of this problem and work together to come up with innovative solutions.

If you're interested in helping build this online community, the best way to start is by requesting an invitation. If you've already submitted a request, you can reach out to John Ellison on Twitter: @iamjohnellison for more information.

If you're interested in working together with people in your local community to help refugees around the world—we'd love to have your help in organizing local meetups for Refugee.Community members.
